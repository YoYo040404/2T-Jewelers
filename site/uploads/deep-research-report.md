Create one polished, mobile-first showcase/catalog microsite for **2T Jewelers**. This is a **design-generation brief**, not a coded wireframe. Make **one strong direction** only.

Use the existing **2T Jewelers logo** as a hard brand anchor. The logo is set on a deep black background, with a bold gold serif wordmark and a stacked monogram where a sculpted gold **T** sits above and overlaps a diamond-filled **2**. Respect that identity, but do **not** make the whole interface look like a heavy 3D beveled logo. Build a cleaner, sharper, more contemporary digital system around it.

## Goal of the page
- Design a **showcase/catalog landing page or microsite** for 2T Jewelers.
- The page must work **right now** as an inquiry-driving destination for visitors coming from Instagram, search, word of mouth, and direct traffic.
- The page should push visitors toward **Instagram, WhatsApp, SMS/text, phone, and DM inquiries**, especially for custom work and high-intent product questions.
- It must also be structured so it can later expand into a full e-commerce system with **collection pages, product pages, reviews, upsells, filters, and checkout** without needing a total redesign.

## Brand and audience context
- The brand sits in the **mid-level / mainstream hip-hop jewelry** space, not ultra-luxury and not cheap dropship energy.
- The audience is style-aware, visually driven, hip-hop and streetwear adjacent, and expects **shine, confidence, and statement pieces**, but still needs **clarity, trust, and easy inquiry paths**.
- The page should feel **bold, urban, glossy, current, and aspirational**, while also feeling like a **real jeweler with real inventory, real service, and real local or personal credibility**.
- The emotional goal on landing: visitors should feel **“this place has the kind of pieces I want, the vibe feels right, and I can reach them fast without guessing.”**
- The cultural alignment should come from **photography, merchandising, rhythm, and confidence**, not from stereotypes, forced slang, graffiti clichés, or fake celebrity posturing.

## Product offering direction
Feature the core assortment that people expect from a strong mid-level hip-hop jewelry business, without making the page feel overcrowded.

Represent these categories clearly:
- **Chains**: Miami Cuban, rope, Franco, tennis, Figaro, herringbone, curb
- **Pendants**: crosses, Jesus pieces, medallions, initials, letters, numbers, name pieces, custom logo/photo pieces
- **Bracelets**: tennis, Cuban, ID-style, bangles where relevant
- **Rings**
- **Earrings / studs**
- **Watches**
- **Grillz**
- **Custom jewelry**

Also represent material and price-tier lanes visually:
- **10K / 14K gold**
- **925 silver**
- **gold-plated / vermeil**
- **moissanite**
- **lab diamond**
- **natural diamond**
- **custom / quote-based pieces**

Merchandising direction:
- Lead with **ready-to-shop categories** first so the site feels broad, browsable, and commercially real.
- Position **custom jewelry** as a premium, aspirational, higher-intent offering that sits alongside the catalog, not separate from it.
- The page should feel like the brand can handle both:
  - **“I want something right now”**
  - **“I want a custom piece made for me”**
- Represent catalog breadth through **category cards, spotlight pieces, material chips, and style filters/chips** rather than dumping dozens of products onto the page.
- Make material and stone distinctions extremely clear. Never blur the difference between **moissanite, lab diamond, natural diamond, silver, gold-plated, and solid gold**.

## Visual direction
Choose a direction that feels like **editorial commerce for hip-hop jewelry**:
- Dark, polished, high-contrast, tactile, product-first
- Rich macro photography, strong lighting, shine, texture, and depth
- Controlled gloss and subtle luxury cues, but not empty ultra-minimal luxury
- Confident and masculine-leaning in structure, but still broadly appealing and not exclusionary
- More **clean power** than chaos
- More **real showroom credibility** than hype graphics

The site should feel like:
- a jeweler that understands the culture
- a local or personal business people can actually contact
- a brand with enough polish to be taken seriously
- a catalog that can scale cleanly

## Color direction
Base the palette on the supplied logo, but modernize it.

Recommended palette direction:
- **True black / near-black** for hero backgrounds and key surfaces
- **Charcoal / graphite / smoked black** for secondary panels and content areas
- **Warm gold / champagne gold** as the main accent
- **Diamond white / cool silver highlights** as a secondary micro-accent for sparkle, edge lighting, or detail emphasis
- **Soft off-white** for readable text

Color strategy:
- Let the **products** bring most of the visual flash.
- Use gold as a **controlled accent**, not as a blanket UI fill.
- Preserve strong contrast and readability everywhere, especially on mobile.

Avoid:
- full-page gold gradients
- loud red sale graphics
- too much pure metallic chrome UI
- tacky glow effects
- oversaturated jewel tones unless used in product imagery only
- making every component black-and-gold in the exact same way

## Typography direction
The existing logo already carries ornate serif energy, so the site typography should balance that with discipline.

Use:
- a **sharp, elegant serif** for hero headlines and select section titles
- a **clean, premium grotesk / neo-grotesk sans** for navigation, UI, product metadata, buttons, and body copy

Typography rules:
- Strong hierarchy
- Tight, intentional spacing
- Good uppercase treatment for labels and chips
- Clear mobile legibility
- Product metadata should feel crisp and organized, not luxury-whispered
- Buttons and CTAs should be instantly readable
- Use the serif sparingly so it feels premium, not old-fashioned

Avoid:
- script fonts
- novelty fonts
- gothic fonts
- condensed all-caps overkill
- tiny low-contrast labels

## Layout and page structure
Design a **single landing page / microsite** with a strong, scroll-based commercial flow.

Header behavior:
- Keep the header clean, premium, and helpful
- Logo on the left
- Tight nav with a few high-utility items only
- One visible inquiry CTA in the header
- Sticky on scroll

Recommended main nav:
- Shop
- Custom
- Best Sellers
- Reviews
- FAQ
- Instagram
- Contact

Do not make the current version feel like a half-built full store. It should feel complete as a **catalog + inquiry experience**.

Footer behavior:
- Dense with useful trust and contact information
- Include contact paths, category links, service links, FAQ, care / policy placeholders, and social access
- Feel grounded, not decorative

## Section-by-section page instructions
Create this section order and make the flow feel deliberate:

### Announcement / utility strip
- A slim top strip for practical trust cues
- Use it for things like verified services and inquiry reassurance
- Examples of the kind of content it should be able to hold: showroom availability, shipping or pickup, financing or layaway if offered, repair service if offered, response speed, custom orders welcome
- Keep it clean and useful, not promo-spammy

### Hero
- Full-width, high-impact, product-led hero
- Use either a dramatic macro product image or a short muted video placeholder showing layered chains, pendants, bracelets, or watches in motion
- The hero should immediately say: this is a real jewelry business with strong style and direct access
- Add a concise headline, a short supporting line, and 2 strong CTAs:
  - **Primary CTA**: inquiry-driven
  - **Secondary CTA**: browse-driven
- The hero should not feel empty; it should feel merchandised and commercial

### Trust / credibility strip
- Directly under the hero
- Use compact badges or short statements for trust cues such as:
  - real materials clarity
  - custom jewelry
  - financing or layaway if available
  - shipping / pickup if available
  - repairs if available
  - local showroom or service-area credibility if relevant
- Keep this visual language restrained and premium

### Featured collections
- A strong category grid that gives immediate structure to the catalog
- Show the core assortment clearly:
  - Chains
  - Pendants
  - Bracelets
  - Rings
  - Earrings
  - Watches
  - Grillz
  - Custom Jewelry
- Each card should feel distinct and desirable
- Use category imagery that reflects the item family, not generic jewelry flatlays

### Best-sellers / spotlight pieces
- Curate a smaller set of standout product cards
- This section should feel shoppable even though the page is inquiry-led
- Feature a mix of:
  - chain staples
  - iced pendants
  - tennis styles
  - watches
  - studs or rings
- If pricing appears, use **clean “from” pricing** for standard items and **“custom quote” / “ask for price”** for one-offs or bespoke pieces
- Avoid giant catalog walls

### Custom orders section
- This must be a major part of the page
- Present custom as premium, exciting, and easy to start
- Show a clear visual process such as:
  1. Send inspiration
  2. Choose metal / stone / style
  3. Review concept
  4. Approve deposit
  5. Production / pickup / shipping
- Include a gallery feel for previous custom-style examples
- Include a custom-specific CTA like:
  - Start Your Custom Piece
  - Text Us Your Idea
  - Send Inspiration on WhatsApp
- This section should feel like a powerful revenue driver, not an afterthought

### Material / product-type highlights
- A visually structured section that helps buyers understand the assortment without turning into a technical essay
- Highlight, for example:
  - Solid Gold
  - 925 Silver
  - Moissanite
  - Lab Diamond
  - Natural Diamond
- Use this section to reinforce transparency, value, and price-tier understanding
- The design should support short explanatory copy and a visual chip/tag system

### Social proof / testimonials / UGC
- Include real-customer energy
- Mix short testimonials, customer photos, pickup moments, close-up wear shots, and social-style proof blocks
- This should feel authentic and current, not corporate
- Keep reviews near the middle of the page, not hidden at the bottom
- Use this section to show that the business is active, responsive, and trusted

### About / credibility strip
- Very concise
- Focus on what makes the business feel real and dependable
- Use themes like:
  - real jeweler, not generic reseller
  - custom work
  - direct communication
  - showroom or local presence if applicable
  - service and responsiveness
- Do not invent heritage stories, celebrity ties, or unsupported claims

### FAQ / trust section
- A compact but high-value FAQ block
- Prioritize questions that reduce friction:
  - How do I start a custom order?
  - What’s the difference between moissanite, lab diamond, and natural diamond?
  - Do you offer financing or layaway?
  - Do you ship or offer pickup?
  - How do grillz fittings work?
  - Can I text photos for a quote?
  - Do you offer repairs?
- Make this section look helpful and premium, not like generic support documentation

### Instagram integration
- Include an Instagram-led gallery or reel-style content block
- It should feel native to a business that is already socially active
- Use it as a credibility and product-discovery section, not a throwaway feed embed
- Focus on:
  - recent drops
  - custom pieces
  - layered looks
  - watches
  - wrist and neck shots
  - customer pickups
- Add a strong CTA to continue browsing or DM on Instagram

### Inquiry / contact section
- This should feel like the conversion close
- Present inquiry methods clearly and visually:
  - Text / SMS
  - WhatsApp
  - Call
  - Instagram DM
  - Short inquiry form
- Make it feel easy to start a conversation without commitment
- Include visual slots for contact details, response expectations, and optional service cues
- Design the form as short and high-intent, not corporate

### Final CTA section
- End with a strong close
- Reassure the user that they can either:
  - ask about a ready-to-buy piece
  - start something custom
- This section should feel bold and simple, with one final main action

## Product presentation style
Product cards must feel premium, legible, and conversion-aware.

Design rules:
- Cards should feel like a hybrid of editorial and commerce
- Use dark or neutral product stages with controlled reflections and shadow depth
- Show 1 primary shot plus room for alternate detail imagery or hover/secondary-view logic
- Use clean metadata structure:
  - product name
  - material / stone type
  - width / size / length where relevant
  - price or inquiry cue
- Use chips for things like:
  - 10K
  - 14K
  - 925
  - Moissanite
  - Lab Diamond
  - Natural Diamond
  - Gold-Plated
  - Custom
- Similar products should be differentiated by:
  - lighting
  - angle
  - styling context
  - scale
  - background treatment
  - visible detail emphasis like clasp, setting, links, stones, or profile thickness

Imagery direction:
- Chains and pendants: mix neck-level lifestyle crops with pure product shots
- Bracelets and watches: wrist shots plus clean product isolations
- Rings: hand shots and macro detail
- Earrings: ear-level crops and clean pair shots
- Grillz: clean detail, fit emphasis, and process-oriented visuals where appropriate
- Use macro detail to show shine, texture, and stone setting quality
- Avoid repetitive floating-product thumbnails that make the catalog feel generic

Catalog feel:
- Fast to scan
- Clearly merchandised
- Broad enough to feel substantive
- Curated enough to feel intentional

## Conversion and CTA system
The site is inquiry-first, not cart-first.

Primary CTA behavior:
- **Start Your Custom Piece**
- **Text for Details**
- **Ask About This Piece**

Secondary CTA behavior:
- **Browse Collections**
- **DM on Instagram**
- **View Recent Work**

Sticky mobile CTA behavior:
- Use a persistent bottom action bar with the highest-utility options:
  - Text
  - WhatsApp
  - Call
  - DM
- Make the icons and labels clear and thumb-friendly
- This should feel useful, not intrusive

Inquiry-method priority:
- **Text / SMS**: best for fast questions, pricing, availability, and quick holds
- **WhatsApp**: best for sharing inspiration photos, video, and back-and-forth custom conversations
- **Instagram DM**: best for social-first shoppers who discovered the brand visually
- **Phone**: best for high-intent buyers, immediate reassurance, and older or local customers
- **Short form**: best as backup for structured custom inquiries or after-hours leads

Friction reduction:
- Use short reassuring copy like “send a photo for a quote” or “ask about this piece”
- Avoid forcing account creation, long forms, or unclear next steps
- Make it obvious that browsing and messaging are both acceptable

- real materials/process clarity
- direct communication cues
- FAQ support

Trust-copy themes:

- responsive communication

- no-pressure inquiry

Do not rely on:

## Mobile behavior
This must be designed mobile-first.

Priorities:
- strong first-screen impact
- fast scanning
- obvious CTAs
- easy thumb use
- clear hierarchy
- no tiny tap targets
- no cramped metallic noise

Mobile rules:
- Use image ratios and card sizes that stay clean on narrow screens
- Prefer stacked or two-up systems that remain readable
- Keep product cards visually rich but compact
- Preserve contrast and spacing
- Keep the sticky CTA bar present but not overwhelming
- Make the mobile menu short, clear, and useful
- Let important actions happen within one or two taps
